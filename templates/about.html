Our Mission

Supporting UN SDG Goal 2: Zero Hunger

Addressing global food waste to create a sustainable, hunger-free community.

The Hunger & Waste Paradox

Globally, about 1.3 billion tons of food are wasted or lost annually—roughly one-third of all food produced for human consumption. At the same time, over 800 million people suffer from chronic undernourishment.

Food waste happens at all stages, from agriculture and processing to retail and households. A major portion of this waste consists of perfectly edible, fresh food from restaurants, hotels, and markets that simply goes unsold.

Did you know?

If we rescue just one-fourth of the food currently lost or wasted globally, it would be enough to feed 870 million hungry people in the world.

What is Zero Hunger?

UN Sustainable Development Goal 2 aims to end hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture by 2030. Key targets include:

  • Ending undernourishment: Ensuring all people have access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food all year round.
  • Ending malnutrition: Eliminating all forms of malnutrition, particularly in children and vulnerable groups.
  • Doubling agricultural productivity: Boosting incomes and productivity of small-scale food producers.
  • Sustainable food production: Supporting agricultural practices that protect ecosystems and strengthen capacity for climate adaptation.

Our Technology in Action

By providing a platform that links surplus food directly to local NGOs and logistics volunteers, we build a resilient, circular economy that addresses local food insecurity while diverting organic waste from landfills.

Zero Waste

Donating excess food avoids the landfill, reducing harmful methane emissions and waste of agricultural resources.

Nutrition

Enables NGOs to access fresh, varied, and nutritious meals, fruits, and bread for distribution.

Community

Builds a collaborative rescue network where businesses, volunteers, and civil society coordinate together.
